# Sovereignty Posture — Three Modes of Exercising Sovereignty

> **scope:** trunk — inherited by all branches

## What Sovereignty Posture Is

node.md says: "a node is sovereign." But sovereignty isn't a single act — it's a posture with three modes. Every branch that interfaces with something external exercises sovereignty in all three modes, at different moments, toward different surfaces.

## The Three Modes

### Defensive Sovereignty

Prevent data, influence, or structural dependency from entering external systems without consent. Block the outflow. Refuse the leak.

The default mode. Most sovereignty acts start here — recognizing that something is flowing out and stopping it.

Examples: ad/tracker blocking, sync disabling, adapter boundaries that keep external vocabulary outside the node, isolation layers that prevent cross-contamination, the FADE membrane that prevents raw entry.

### Extractive Sovereignty

Pull data, resources, or knowledge back from external systems after the fact. Recover what's already out there. Repatriate.

The reactive mode. Something is already in someone else's system — pull it back into yours.

Examples: data exports and takeouts, archive requests, conversation extraction from AI platforms, the FADE protocol's Find phase pulling external resources through the airlock.

### Assertive Sovereignty

Claim ownership at the moment of generation, before the ownership question is settled. The record originates in your system; external systems get a copy (or don't).

The mature mode. Not reacting to what platforms do with your data, but proactively establishing your record as primary.

Examples: intercepting engagement signals at click time (your like is recorded in your ecology before the platform processes it), originating creative work locally and publishing copies outward, your intent documents being the primary record from which agent behavior derives.

## The Posture as a Whole

The three modes aren't a progression (you don't graduate from defensive to assertive). They operate simultaneously, at different surfaces, in every branch. A single session might block a tracker (defensive), ingest browser history (extractive), and capture an engagement signal (assertive).

The posture is the combination. Full sovereignty means all three modes are active across all surfaces where your data, attention, or creative output meets external systems.

## Relationship to Other Trunk Protocols

- **FADE** is primarily extractive (Find phase pulls in) with a defensive component (the airlock membrane prevents raw entry).
- **Noise floor** is defensive — attenuating irrelevant signal prevents noise from consuming sovereign attention.
- **Procedural memory** is assertive — the ecology's accumulated knowledge is the primary record of what works and fails.
- **Opportunistic separation** is assertive — recognizing a separation and standing up a project IS the sovereign claim.
- **Contextual providence** is assertive — patterns are providentially available through the trunk because the ecology asserts its own cross-branch intelligence.
