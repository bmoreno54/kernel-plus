# First Session Protocol

> You're joining a game in progress. This document gets you from
> clone to first productive session in under 10 minutes.

## What You Just Cloned

A sovereign ecology seed — structural commitments and operational
protocols for building a personal knowledge ecosystem that grows from
your own friction. Five positions tell you what things ARE. Twelve
protocols tell you how things MOVE. Domain templates show you what
working looks like. 156 harvested atoms give you intellectual fuel
from day one.

Nothing here tells you what to build. Everything here tells you how
to build anything.

## Step 1: Claim the Alpha Entity (2 minutes)

Open `alpha_entity.md`. Replace the template with yourself.

This is not a user profile. It is a sovereign declaration: you are
the zero-class entity. Every node in this ecology exists in relation
to you. Agents work FOR this node.

Minimum viable claim:
- Your name
- Your relationship to tools (do you hold the means of production?)
- What surfaces you currently operate across (devices, platforms, identities)

The rest accretes through use. Don't overthink it.

## Step 2: Pick a Domain, Run FADE (5 minutes)

Look at `seeds/`. Pick the template closest to something you're
actually working on right now. Copy it to a new top-level directory:

```
cp seeds/tech.md my-project/README.md
```

Now run FADE on one real thing:

1. **Find** — identify one external resource, tool, or body of
   knowledge relevant to your project
2. **Analyze** — what does it actually say/do? (not what you
   assume)
3. **Decompose** — break it into atoms. What are the load-bearing
   parts?
4. **Exploit** — what can you do with those atoms in your context?

Write the result as a markdown file in your project directory.
Tag it: `#fade #research #[your-domain-tags]`.

You just did the fundamental operation. Everything else is this,
at different scales.

## Step 3: Commit (30 seconds)

```
git add -A
git commit -m "first fade: [what you processed]"
```

The ecology is now alive. It has one node (your alpha entity), one
project, and one piece of metabolized knowledge. Everything from
here is accretion.

## What Happens Next

### The ecology grows when you work

Every time you process external material → FADE it.
Every time a project develops distinct concerns → separate it.
Every time you notice a cross-cutting pattern → tag it.
Every time something fails → record it in procedural memory.

### The protocols are tools, not rules

Read them as needed. `trunk/fade.md` when you're ingesting.
`trunk/omnibus.md` when you're wondering how to organize.
`trunk/procedural_memory.md` when something went wrong.
`trunk/rendering.md` when you're building a surface that shows
collections of things.

You don't need to memorize them. They're reference material
that becomes intuitive through use.

### The harvests are fuel

`harvests/` contains 156 domain-independent structural insights.
Browse them when you want intellectual stimulation or when you're
stuck. They're organized by theme. Each one is a single sentence
that should resonate across whatever domain you're working in.

### Develop your own protocols

The trunk protocols evolved from someone else's friction. They'll
serve you well as starting points. But your friction is different.
When you discover operational patterns that keep working, write
them down. When they prove themselves across multiple projects,
promote them to your trunk.

### Compressed triggers

As you work, you'll develop shorthand — compressed instructions
that encode complex operational protocols into minimal notation.
These are personal. They ritualize through use. When you find
yourself repeating the same multi-sentence instruction to agents,
compress it into a trigger and document it in your omnibus.

## The Only Rule

The ecology serves you. If something isn't working, change it.
If a protocol creates friction without value, modify or discard it.
If the structure gets in the way of the work, the work wins.

The kernel is tested and general. But YOUR ecology is sovereign.
It becomes yours through use, not through compliance.
