# node

a node is sovereign. it can be read alone and understood as what it is.

a node describes what it is and what it needs. it does not describe
how its needs are met, or speak in the vocabulary of whatever meets them.

a node carries its own provenance: who, when, from what. not narrated —
structural.

a node declares its relations outward. the relations are typed and
directional, but the types are not prescribed — they emerge from what
the node actually relates to and how.

a node that contains another system's language inside its own body
has lost sovereignty. the translation happens at the boundary, not
inside the node.

these are the invariants. everything else — what types of nodes exist,
how they're formatted, what sections they contain — is emergent. each
node discovers its own shape by being what it is.
