## Accumulation & Identity

**Compositional identity emerges from modifier layering, not fixed types.** A base entity plus accumulated modifier tags creates emergent identity that neither component contains alone. Identity-through-composition rather than identity-through-classification.

**Composites crystallize when constituent conditions converge, without being pre-authored.** Identity, reputation, and status often crystallize without anyone designing them. When enough dimensions of behavior converge past a threshold, a recognizable pattern appears — not by design but by convergence.

**Origin never fades — it composes perpetually with acquired context.** Where you came from does not wash out. It continues to interact with where you are.

**Naming grows through use, not upfront classification.** Names that grow bidirectionally through practice (toward generality and toward specificity) carry richer information than names assigned at birth.

**Depth produces qualitative shifts, not just quantitative increases.** Accumulated experience doesn't just make things harder; it changes what kind of thing you are dealing with. Phase transitions, not gradients.

**Diminishing returns on repetition is system health, not a bug.** Any system that rewards pure repetition without diminishment is exploitable and collapses toward degenerate strategies.

## Sovereignty & Boundaries

**Three sovereignty modes: defensive, extractive, assertive.** Systems that interface with external entities exercise all three simultaneously. Defensive prevents outflow. Extractive recovers what's already out. Assertive claims ownership at moment of generation.

**The convenience-control fault line.** Tool ecosystems contain a hidden split: tools where the platform holds the means of production vs tools where you hold them. The more valuable the artifact, the more this distinction matters. Can you leave?

**The socket model.** Design external dependencies as constitutional positions any compatible provider can occupy. The current occupant is not the architecture; it is the current tenant.

**Trust shatters, it doesn't degrade.** Trust accumulates slowly and collapses catastrophically. The asymmetry between accumulation rate and destruction rate is fundamental. Systems modeling trust as a bidirectional meter miss the structural fragility.

**Vocabulary lock-in is capability, not constraint.** Specialized vocabulary creates lock-in but also creates higher-bandwidth communication. The design question: at what level does the vocabulary bind? Protocol-level agnosticism can coexist with content-level specificity.

## Perception & Knowledge

**Knowledge accumulates through observation, not declaration.** Experiential knowledge (confidence built through repeated contact) is structurally different from declarative knowledge.

**Perception-as-parameter changes the entire ontology, not just the interface.** How much you can perceive changes what reality you live in. Two entities with different perceptual capacities inhabit different worlds.

**The gap between intensity and comprehension is the design space.** At peak moments, intensity outrunns understanding. The space between "this is happening" and "I understand what this is" is where the most important work occurs.

**Every action has a perceptual signature that radiates into the environment.** Nothing is invisible. Every action broadcasts. The question is never "did I act undetected" but "what was the signature."

## Systems & Failure

**Failure has exactly two structural modes: over-crystallization and over-dissolution.** Systems fail by becoming too rigid or too chaotic. A variant: dissolution toward a patron (losing your own structure while serving another's). Health is the active oscillation, not a static midpoint.

**Corruption is continuous pressure, not a binary state change.** Compromise, burnout, decay — none are events. They are accumulations. Systems that model degradation as a state change detect it too late.

**Failure is a narrative transition, not a state reset.** Failure doesn't return to zero. It transitions into a different frame, a different relationship, a different mode.

**Capture is the universal antagonist.** The force that flattens local sovereignty into uniform compliance exists everywhere — monopoly, hegemony, ossification, homogenization. It is domain-independent.

**Immune memory through accumulated failure records.** Recording failures and diagnoses as persistent context creates an immune system. Learning is in the context assembly, not in any individual component.

## Composition & Architecture

**A small set of primitives serving a vast composite space beats a large set of specialized components.** Feature creep is resisted by insisting new capabilities emerge from existing primitives at new scales.

**Self-similarity across scales is both aesthetic principle and architectural constraint.** Systems that behave the same way at every level are learnable: understanding one level teaches all levels.

**Three perspectives on the same thing are one thing from three angles.** Convergence across disciplines is not synthesis of different ideas but recognition that one idea was always there.

**A group is more than the sum of its pairwise relationships.** Group-level properties emerge that no dyadic analysis captures. Geometric means of diverse profiles produce properties no individual contains.

**Composition by cascade, delegation, and stigmergy.** Sub-domain composition follows three paradigms: cascade (inherit and override), delegation (explicit authority grant), stigmergy (signals accumulate through use). Most real systems need a hybrid.

## Process & Method

**The specification is the real artifact; executable code is disposable.** When intent-specifications are primary and implementation is derivative, the meaning layer becomes durable and implementation becomes regenerable.

**Middle-out development: specification and implementation enrich each other bidirectionally.** Neither layer is authority; both co-develop. Distinct from both top-down and bottom-up.

**Testing as trajectory, not state verification.** Launch a process and observe its full arc. The test succeeds when the trajectory is coherent. Validates dynamics, not statics. Naturally discovers emergent properties.

**Ingestion as a phase cycle: Find-Analyze-Decompose-Exploit.** The FADE protocol generalized: how any system metabolizes foreign material into native knowledge.

**Context is produced, not given.** Context actively machined by the consuming entity from its current situation. A system where context arrives pre-assembled is one where the receiver has been objectified.

**For singular/immediate needs, direct acquisition beats automation; for categorical/ongoing needs, automation wins.** The appropriate tool depends on whether the need is one-shot or recurring.

## Environment & Persistence

**Space itself carries an intensity gradient that modulates everything within it.** Environments are not neutral containers. Ambient context sets a floor for the intensity of everything that happens within it.

**Sustained emission reshapes the field around the emitter.** A person who holds a sustained posture continuously modifies the environment. Fundamentally different from discrete intervention.

**Persistence is a gradient with different decay rates at different layers.** Not binary (saved/unsaved). Some things persist forever, some across related contexts, some are volatile. Any system treating persistence as on/off loses temporal structure.

**Autonomous entities accumulate state whether or not anyone is observing.** The world does not pause when you look away. Systems assuming nothing changes without observation are modeling a world that doesn't exist.

**The dead continue to influence through transformed relationship.** Loss doesn't delete relationship. It transforms it. Absent entities persist as shaped absences that modulate present behavior.

## Human-Agent Collaboration

**The harnessed human is the source of all general intelligence.** The system captures and structures energy but never generates it. The human provides vitality, direction, judgment.

**The system adapts to the human, not the human to the system.** When a framework is truly general, different users produce different surface expressions of the same structural needs.

**Personality under pressure is revealed by deviation from pattern.** We are most legible when we act against type. The deviation from established pattern is the signal, and the friction of that deviation is consequential.

**Shared vulnerability creates bonds that shared success does not.** The moments that build the deepest relationships are exposures, not victories.

**Permission to hold contradictions simultaneously is a generative capacity.** Forcing resolution prematurely kills generative tension. Negative capability is a capacity, not a deficiency.

## Meta-Kernel

**The kernel is a property (domain-independence), not a location.** Core principles are not where they are filed but what survives removal of context.

**Stripping context reveals structure invisible while embedded.** Abstraction is not just removal; it is revelation.

**Generality is tested by a different person producing different results from the same seed.** Self-application proves nothing about the methodology; it might prove things about the inventor.

**Fork adaptations prove the original by showing what varies.** Any system claiming universality is tested by applying it in a different context and seeing what survives unchanged.

**Constraint amplifies rather than limits.** True of any creative medium. Limited expression surfaces force compositional depth.

**Kayfabe — collaborative commitment to treating constructed systems as meaningful — is how meaning emerges from mechanics.** The gap between "this is just numbers" and "this matters" is bridged by commitment, not by the numbers becoming magical.

**Implicit patterns become powerful when named explicitly.** The act of naming what was already happening — giving it grammar — is how tacit knowledge becomes transferable.
